Printable Materials & Instructions – Week 1: Renaissance & Reformation

1. Renaissance vs. Medieval Art Comparison Worksheet

Purpose: Help students identify features of Renaissance art by contrasting with Medieval styles.

  • Includes 3 image pairs with guided questions:
  • "What do you notice about light and depth?"
  • "How realistic are the people?"
  • "What values does the image reflect?"

Instructions: Print double-sided or distribute digitally. Encourage annotations using icons or color coding.

2. Renaissance City Map Project

Purpose: Engage students visually by designing a fictional Renaissance city with labeled features:

  • Humanist school
  • Artist studio
  • Printing press
  • Marketplace
  • Church or cathedral

Instructions: Print a blank map or allow digital drawing. Add brief captions to explain building purpose.

3. Martin Luther’s 95 Theses – Excerpt Sheet

Purpose: Help students analyze Reformation themes using 6–8 simplified excerpts.

  • "What is he accusing the Church of?"
  • "What change does he want?"

Instructions: Distribute and annotate in class or pairs. Highlight repeated concerns.

4. Debate Role Cards – Reformation Town Hall

Roles:

  • Peasant
  • Local printer
  • Church official
  • German noble
  • Young student

Instructions: Print and cut cards. Assign randomly or by choice. Give 5–10 minutes prep before debate.

5. Self-Guided Worksheet Options

  • Venn Diagram – Renaissance vs. Reformation
  • Illustrated 1-pager of Luther’s complaints
  • Chart comparing art and reform values

Instructions: Let students choose format. Accept visual, written, or blended work.
