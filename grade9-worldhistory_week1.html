Week 1: Renaissance & Reformation

Unit Theme: Ideas, Power, and Transformation in Early Modern Europe

Session 1: The Renaissance

  • Topics: Humanism, classical revival, Renaissance art, major city-states
  • Key Figures: Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Petrarch, Medici family
  • Activities: Art analysis, "Renaissance City Map" project
  • Essential Question: How did Renaissance thinking challenge the medieval worldview?

Session 2: The Reformation

  • Topics: Martin Luther, the 95 Theses, the printing press, Church corruption
  • Key Figures: Martin Luther, Pope Leo X, John Calvin
  • Activities: Primary source analysis of Luther’s Theses, "Reform vs. Tradition" debate
  • Essential Question: Why did people begin to question the authority of the Church?

Session 3 (Self-Guided): Art & Reform Inquiry

  • Option 1: Compare and contrast Medieval vs. Renaissance paintings (worksheet)
  • Option 2: Create a short illustrated booklet or Google Slides on Luther’s key complaints
  • Prompt: "What did the Renaissance and Reformation have in common? Where did they clash?"

Recommended Videos

  • Crash Course: The Renaissance (11 min)
  • Leonardo da Vinci and the Renaissance – PBS (6 min)
  • Crash Course: The Protestant Reformation (15 min)
  • Luther and the 95 Theses – PBS (8 min)

Extension Activities & Projects

  • Art Analysis: Use a side-by-side worksheet comparing paintings across periods, focusing on emotion, realism, and symbolism.
  • City Map Project: Design a fictional Renaissance city with labeled features: press, cathedral, art studio, humanist school.
  • Debate Roleplay: Prepare roles for Socratic debate – noble, peasant, Pope’s ally, printer – using sentence stems.
  • Venn Diagram Option: Compare Renaissance and Reformation under themes: Reason, Faith, Corruption, Power.
