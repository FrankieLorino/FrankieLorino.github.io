Teacher Guide – Week 1: Renaissance & Reformation

Grade Level: 10
Unit Theme: Ideas, Power, and Transformation in Early Modern Europe
Week Focus: Renaissance & Reformation
Sessions per week: 3 (2 guided + 1 self-guided)

Session 1: The Renaissance

  • Objective: Understand how Renaissance art, philosophy, and science challenged medieval norms.
  • Key Topics: Humanism, classical revival, city-states, da Vinci, Michelangelo, Medici
  • Discussion Prompts:
    • "Why did people start valuing human reason and beauty more during this time?"
    • "What’s one thing you see in Renaissance art that you don’t see in medieval art?"
  • Activities: Art comparison, Renaissance city map (include a key)

Session 2: The Reformation

  • Objective: Explore how the Reformation challenged religious and political power structures.
  • Key Topics: Martin Luther, the 95 Theses, printing press, Church corruption
  • Discussion Prompts:
    • "Why would regular people support Luther’s challenge?"
    • "If you were a peasant reading Luther’s ideas for the first time, how would you feel?"
  • Activity: Roleplay town hall meeting: printer, noble, peasant, church official

Session 3: Self-Guided

  • Worksheet comparing medieval vs. Renaissance paintings
  • Create illustrated Slides or booklets of Luther’s complaints
  • Venn diagram comparing values in Renaissance vs. Reformation
  • Prompt: “What do these movements have in common? Where do they clash?”

Recommended Media

  • Crash Course: The Renaissance
  • PBS: Leonardo da Vinci
  • Crash Course: Protestant Reformation
  • PBS: Martin Luther’s 95 Theses

Teaching Tips (ADHD-Friendly)

  • Use visual anchors: bold icons, diagrams, checklists
  • Offer multiple response formats: drawing, writing, speaking
  • Break tasks into chunks with visible progress steps
  • Use sentence stems: "I noticed..." "This reminds me of..."

Materials Checklist

  • Materials
  • Art comparison worksheet (images included)
  • Excerpt sheet of 95 Theses
  • Debate/roleplay cards: peasant, noble, pope, printer, etc.
  • Markers or colored pencils for map/drawing activities

Mastery Checks

  • Can students identify features of Renaissance art and thought?
  • Can they explain why Luther challenged Church authority?
  • Can they summarize how both movements reshaped power?
  • Have they completed a visual or written response with reflection?

You're not just teaching history—you're teaching students to think boldly about ideas, voices, and change.
